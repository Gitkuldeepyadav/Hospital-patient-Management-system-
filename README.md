# Hospital-patient-Management-system
For this project, I wanted to simulate how a real hospital stores and analyzes data to ensure patient safety and optimize its resources. 

## Step 1: Database Design
"I started by designing a relational database with 7 interconnected tables. This allowed me to map the complete patient journey, tracking everything from their initial appointment scheduling down to their diagnosis, treatment timeline, and final billing."  

## Step 2: Data Analysis
"Once the database structure was built, I executed over 30 complex SQL queries using multiple table joins and aggregations. My focus wasn't just on fetching data, but on finding actionable insights to improve hospital administration."  

## Step 3: Key Insights
"Through these queries, I was able to deliver several major insights:"

### Workload Management:
"I ranked doctors based on the number of appointments they handled to help administration balance doctor workloads effectively."  

### Operational Efficiency: 
"I calculated the average treatment duration for each department to identify where patients were spending the most time and where the hospital could speed up processes." 

### Financial Health: 
"I tracked unpaid bills and patients with pending dues to help the hospital improve its cash flow, and I generated daily revenue reports."  

### Resource Planning: 
"I also identified the most frequently occurring medical diagnoses. This is crucial because it helps the hospital know exactly which medicines to keep in stock and which specialists are needed the most."
